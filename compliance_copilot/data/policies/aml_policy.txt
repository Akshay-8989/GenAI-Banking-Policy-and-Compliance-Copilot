1. INTRODUCTION

Money laundering is the process of making illegally obtained money appear legitimate.
SecureBank is committed to complying with all applicable AML laws and regulations.
This policy establishes procedures to detect, prevent, and report money laundering
and related financial crimes.

2. TRANSACTION MONITORING AND REPORTING THRESHOLDS

2.1 Cash Transaction Reports (CTR)

All cash transactions above the following thresholds must be reported to the
Financial Intelligence Unit – India (FIU-IND):

- INR 10,00,000 (10 lakhs) or equivalent in foreign currency in a single transaction
- Multiple cash transactions by a single customer in a day totalling INR 10 lakhs
- All cross-border wire transfers of INR 5 lakhs and above

Reporting Deadline: Within 7 working days from the last day of the month in which
the transaction occurred.

2.2 Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR)

A Suspicious Transaction Report must be filed when:
- The transaction has no apparent economic justification
- The customer is reluctant to provide identification
- Unusual patterns inconsistent with the customer's profile
- Transactions involving jurisdictions known for money laundering

STR Filing Deadline: Within 24 hours of detection of the suspicious transaction,
irrespective of the transaction amount.

2.3 Non-Profit Organisation Transactions (NPO)

Transactions with non-profit organisations above INR 5 lakhs must be reported
if there is suspicion of terrorist financing or misuse of funds.

3. RED FLAGS AND SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY INDICATORS

The following are indicators of potential money laundering:

3.1 Customer Behaviour Red Flags
- Customer provides inconsistent or false information
- Customer is evasive about the source of funds
- Customer requests to break down large transactions to avoid reporting
- Customer shows unusual interest in bank's reporting requirements
- Customer appears nervous or in a hurry to complete transactions

3.2 Transaction Red Flags
- Sudden large cash deposits with no business justification
- Multiple transfers to/from high-risk countries
- Rapid movement of funds (same day credit and debit)
- Round-number transactions repeatedly (e.g., exactly 9,99,000)
- Deposits followed by immediate full withdrawal
- Use of multiple accounts to funnel funds

3.3 Business Account Red Flags
- Business transacts in sectors inconsistent with its registration
- Turnover inconsistent with the nature of the business
- Large number of incoming transfers from unrelated third parties
- Payments to suppliers in high-risk jurisdictions without clear business reason

4. SANCTIONS SCREENING

4.1 Screening Requirements

All customers and transactions must be screened against:
- UN Security Council Sanctions Lists
- RBI Consolidated Sanctions List
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions List
- Domestic terrorist designation lists under UAPA

4.2 Screening Frequency
- New customers: Before account opening
- Existing customers: Daily batch screening
- Transactions: Real-time screening for all wire transfers above INR 50,000

4.3 Hit Management

Positive matches must be escalated to the Compliance team within 1 hour.
Accounts with confirmed sanctions matches must be frozen immediately and
reported to RBI within 24 hours.

5. POLITICALLY EXPOSED PERSONS (PEP)

5.1 Definition

A Politically Exposed Person (PEP) is an individual who holds or has held a
prominent public function, including:
- Heads of state or government
- Senior politicians and government officials
- Senior judicial officials
- Senior executives of state-owned corporations
- High-ranking military officials

Family members and close associates of PEPs are treated as PEPs.

5.2 PEP Handling Procedures
- Senior management approval required before opening PEP account
- Enhanced due diligence at onboarding and every 2 years thereafter
- Source of wealth declaration mandatory
- All transactions above INR 1 lakh require Relationship Manager review
- Ongoing adverse media monitoring

6. EMPLOYEE TRAINING AND AWARENESS

All bank employees must complete:
- AML Foundation Training: Within 30 days of joining
- Annual AML Refresher Training: Every calendar year
- Role-specific training for Compliance, Operations, and Relationship Managers
- Certification must be maintained in employee records

Failure to complete mandatory AML training is a disciplinary offence.

7. TIPPING OFF PROHIBITION

It is a criminal offence to inform (tip off) a customer or any third party that:
- A suspicious transaction report has been filed about them
- They are under investigation for money laundering

Employees who tip off customers face immediate termination and criminal prosecution
under Section 8A of the Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002.

8. RECORD RETENTION

All AML records including STRs, CTRs, and transaction monitoring records must be
retained for a minimum of 5 years from the date of the report or transaction,
whichever is later. Records must be available to regulatory authorities within
2 working days of a request.
