1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This document outlines the Know Your Customer (KYC) policy for SecureBank. KYC
is a mandatory regulatory process that requires financial institutions to verify
the identity of their clients. This policy is applicable to all branches, subsidiaries,
and employees of SecureBank and covers all customer types including individuals,
corporates, and institutional clients.

2. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

This policy is governed by the following regulatory guidelines:
- Reserve Bank of India (RBI) Master Direction on KYC, 2016 (updated 2023)
- Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002
- Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999
- Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendations
- Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) KYC Regulations

3. CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION DOCUMENTS

3.1 Individual Customers

All individual customers must submit the following Officially Valid Documents (OVDs):

Identity Proof (any one):
- Passport (valid, not expired)
- Aadhaar Card (12-digit UIDAI number)
- Voter Identity Card
- Driving License (valid)
- NREGA Job Card
- National Population Register letter

Address Proof (any one, dated within 3 months):
- Utility bill (electricity, water, telephone)
- Bank account statement from another bank
- Letter from a gazetted officer confirming address
- Property/municipal tax receipt

Financial Proof:
- PAN Card (mandatory for all transactions above INR 50,000)
- Form 60/61 if PAN is not available

3.2 Corporate/Business Customers

For companies and business entities, the following documents are required:
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Memorandum and Articles of Association
- Board resolution authorising account opening
- List of authorised signatories with specimen signatures
- PAN of the entity
- Address proof of registered office
- KYC documents of all beneficial owners holding 25% or more stake
- Latest audited financial statements

3.3 Non-Resident Indians (NRI)

NRI customers must provide:
- Valid passport with visa/residency permit
- Overseas address proof
- Indian address proof (if available)
- PAN card or Form 60

4. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)

4.1 Standard CDD

Standard Customer Due Diligence applies to all low-risk and medium-risk customers:
- Verify customer identity using OVDs
- Understand the purpose and nature of the business relationship
- Conduct ongoing monitoring of the account
- Periodic KYC refresh every 10 years for low-risk, 8 years for medium-risk

4.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Enhanced Due Diligence is mandatory for high-risk customers including:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their family members
- Customers in high-risk geographies as notified by FATF
- Non-face-to-face customers
- Customers with complex ownership structures
- Those with adverse media coverage or regulatory actions

EDD measures include:
- Senior management approval for account opening
- Additional identification documents
- Source of funds/wealth declaration
- More frequent monitoring (every 2 years)
- Transaction pattern analysis

4.3 Simplified CDD

Simplified KYC applies only to:
- Small accounts (transactions under INR 50,000 per year)
- Jan Dhan Yojana accounts
- Accounts opened under PMJDY scheme

5. RECORD KEEPING AND RETENTION

5.1 Retention Period

KYC documents must be retained for:
- Minimum 5 years from the date of account closure
- Minimum 5 years from the date of the transaction for occasional customers
- All records must be readily retrievable within 2 working days

5.2 Format Requirements

Records may be maintained in:
- Original physical documents
- Certified true copies
- Digital/scanned copies with electronic signature

5.3 Data Security

- All KYC data must be stored in encrypted format
- Access restricted to authorised compliance and operations staff
- CCTV footage at branches to be retained for minimum 6 months

6. KYC REFRESH AND PERIODIC UPDATION

Customer KYC must be refreshed at the following intervals:
- Low Risk: Every 10 years
- Medium Risk: Every 8 years
- High Risk: Every 2 years

Triggers for immediate KYC refresh:
- Significant change in transaction pattern
- Customer reported in adverse media
- Request from law enforcement agencies
- Account dormancy exceeding 2 years

7. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

Non-compliance with KYC regulations may result in:
- Monetary penalties up to INR 1 crore per violation under PMLA
- Suspension or cancellation of banking license
- Personal liability for Compliance Officer and senior management
- Regulatory action by RBI including directions, restrictions, and fines
- Criminal prosecution under PMLA 2002 (imprisonment up to 7 years)

8. RESPONSIBILITIES

Compliance Officer: Overall responsibility for KYC policy implementation
Branch Manager: Ensure all accounts comply with KYC norms
Operations Team: Document collection, verification, and storage
IT Department: Maintain secure systems for KYC data management
Internal Audit: Annual review of KYC compliance
